The Vital Tradition of Naming Baby Gorillas in Rwanda

A baby gorilla seen during the Kwita Izina ceremony in Rwanda's Volcanoes National Park.

Every year, Rwanda celebrates the Kwita Izina ceremony, a uniquely Rwandan event where newborn gorillas are given names in a celebration inspired by traditional baby-naming customs. It’s a festive occasion that brings together conservationists, locals, and global travelers in the heart of Africa.

The ceremony is not only a symbolic gesture but also plays a real role in conservation efforts. By naming the gorillas and monitoring them closely, Rwanda has become a leader in protecting this endangered species, and the population has steadily increased thanks to this commitment.
