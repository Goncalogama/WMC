The Vital Tradition of Naming Baby Gorillas in Rwanda

A baby gorilla seen during the Kwita Izina ceremony in Rwanda's Volcanoes National Park.

In the heart of Rwanda’s misty mountains, an extraordinary tradition unites conservation, culture, and community: the annual Kwita Izina ceremony, where baby gorillas are officially named. Modeled on age-old Rwandan naming customs, this event has become a symbol of Rwanda’s world-renowned conservation strategy.

What began in 2005 has grown into an internationally celebrated occasion, attracting conservationists, celebrities, and travelers alike. With every name given, Rwanda strengthens its commitment to monitoring and protecting its most iconic inhabitants—mountain gorillas.

Cultural Meaning, Global Impact

Each name carries meaning—drawn from Rwandan values, the gorilla’s behavior, or conservation achievements. The ceremony is more than symbolic; it supports real-world tracking and management of gorilla families, which has helped increase the population steadily over the years.

Kwita Izina Through the Years:
  • 2005 – First official ceremony held with support from Rwanda Development Board
  • 2010 – Live broadcasting of the event begins
  • 2020 – Ceremony adapted during the COVID-19 pandemic
  • 2023 – Global figures and virtual naming included for greater outreach

The Ceremony Experience

Visitors lucky enough to attend in person witness traditional drumming, storytelling, and conservation talks, all set against the backdrop of Rwanda’s lush highlands. Visuals from the event are breathtaking—newborn gorillas nestled in their mothers' arms, dignitaries in vibrant attire, and rangers proudly announcing each new name.

Looking Ahead

Kwita Izina is a celebration of life, heritage, and sustainability. It shows the power of storytelling and identity in conservation. With Rwanda’s commitment and global collaboration, this tradition will likely continue to inspire—and protect—for generations to come.
